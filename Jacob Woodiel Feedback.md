## CDPS

### Leader - Project Leadership

_Keep developing a consistent habit of checking in as you work. You already give clear updates in standups and when prompted. As a next step, build a habit of sharing progress at the points you commit to, like after a time box or at meaningful milestones. This helps the team stay aligned, prevents falling into rabbit holes, and creates more opportunities for support when something needs more time. Use feedback loops with your supervisor to determine the right cadence._

### Professional - Communication

_Expand the effectiveness of your communication by matching the message to the right channel. Before reaching out, think about what you need to accomplish and which venue will support that goal. For example, a quick Teams message is great for fast alignment, a call can help resolve something urgent or nuanced, and email works well when a decision needs to have an audit trail. Continue checking your choice with your manager and mentor so you can expand your sense of when each approach is most effective._

- 
    

---

### Architect - Problem Solving

_Build comfort navigating ambiguous tasks by following your curiosity to shape workable solutions. You’ve shown you can deliver complicated features when outcomes are clear, even if the implementation details aren’t fully defined. The next step is to apply that same ownership in situations where the problem itself is still taking shape by asking questions, exploring context, and clarifying what matters. For example, understanding the business purpose behind a system and how a task fits within it can help you make better decisions and move forward with confidence in more ambiguous situations._
